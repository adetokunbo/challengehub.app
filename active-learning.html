Challenge-Based Active Learning

How it works

The principles of Challenge-Based Active Learning

Learning focusses around a series of challenges, with each challenge addressing a concept.

Each challenge builds upon understanding gained from previous challenges.

Students spend more time on challenges they find difficult and less time on those they find easy.

Class time consists of discussion between students, and optionally a short lecture or demonstration by the teacher.

The nature of the challenge

Unlike traditional flipped-classroom models, study focusses on the challenge, not the reading. This gives students clear motivation to complete assigned tasks.

With relevant resources included by the teacher, students learn valuable self-study habits in a structured environment.

In contrast to traditional homework, the student can immediately check their answer, keeping them in the flow when it's wrong and providing a motivational win when it's right.

The nature of the class

Challenge-hub automatically pairs students based on their performance, enabling personalised instruction as students ask each other questions, discuss difficult concepts and learn how to explain ideas.

The teacher can still choose to lecture to introduce a topic, motivate students or otherwise address a class-wide issue.

The teacher walks into the classroom knowing the progress of the class and what questions are likely to come up, making for efficient class-preparation and student learning.

Implementation models

Full Challenge-Based Active Learning

Assessment model

  • Final exam.
  • Challenge logbook submitted at the end of the semester showing calculations for completion of most challenges.

Class preparation

  • Review challenge performance since the last class.
  • Optionally, prepare a short explanation covering common difficulties.
  • Prepare new challenges for release at the beginning of class.

Class time

  • Perform a short lecture, perhaps covering a common topic of difficulty or performing a demonstration to motivate students about a new topic.
  • Peer discussion for the remainder of the class.
  • Students who have finished discussing the previous week's challenges can start working on the new challenges.
  • The teacher walks around the classroom, discussing with students and answering questions as required.

Testing Challenge-Based Active Learning in your traditional lecture-based course

A traditional lecture except...

  • Replace some lecture-based information delivery with challenge-motivated self-study.
  • Utilise the class-time gained for student peer-discussion.
  • Use Challenge-Hub to understand what students found difficult and cover it in your lecture.
  • After some weeks, ask the students what they think about the change of learning style!

Model-answer tutorial classes

Traditional style

  • Students work through given questions, then the teacher goes through one or more model answers at the end of the class.

Challenge-Based Active Learning tutorial style

  • Deliver tutorial questions through Challenge-Hub.
  • Get real-time feedback about tutorial-question completion, difficulty and comments from students.
  • Have Challenge-Hub automatically pair students so that they can most help each other with personalised explanations.
  • At end of class, target explanation of the model answer on the question that students found most difficult.
