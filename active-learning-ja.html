チャレンジ駆動アクティブラーニング

How it works

The principle of Challenge-Based Active Learning

Challenge-Based Active Learning (CBAL) focusses around a series of challenges, with each challenge addressing a concept.

Each challenge builds upon understanding gained from previous challenges.

Students spend more time on challenges they find difficult and less time on those they find easy.

Class time consists of discussion between students, and optionally a short lecture or demonstration by the teacher.

The nature of the challenge

Students will often not read background material unless the motivation is clear. Unlike traditional flipped-classroom models, CBAL focusses on the challenges, not the reference material.

The teacher can suggest reference resources, and the student may choose to use them or use other resources, even sharing them with the teacher or class if desired.

In contrast to traditional homework, with enhanced answer-checking the student can immediately check their answer, keeping them in the flow when it's wrong and providing a motivational win when it's right.

The nature of the class

Discussion between students in class is an integral part of CBAL. Challenge-hub automatically pairs students based on their performance, enabling peer-instruction as students discuss difficult concepts and ideas with each other.

The teacher walks into the classroom knowing the progress of the class and what questions are likely to come up, making for efficient class-preparation and student learning.

The teacher can still lecture to introduce a topic, motivate students or otherwise address a class-wide issue.
